HOTKEYS

Key (case-sensitive) Output Meaning
^ ^ exponent, or "to the power of."
i ^-1 inverse, or 1 / \< the previous number\>.
s ^ sine.
c ^ cosine.
t tan tangent.
S sin^-1 arcsine, inverse sine, or anti-sine.
C cos^-1 arccosine, inverse cosine, or anti-cosine.
T tan^-1 arctangent, inverse tangent, or anti-tangent.
d N/A changes mode from degrees to radians, or radians to degree, as indicated just above the buttons, affecting trigonometric operations appropriately.
p π π, the ratio between a circle's circumfrence and its diameter (about 3.14). Also the Math God of Magic.
e e e, the natural number. Also the Math Goddess of Mystery.
l log Common logarithm, or the logarithm of base-10.
n ln Natural logarithm, or the logarithm of base-e.
a ans The answer to the above expression. If the above expression is invalid, then this one will be as well.
↑ (up-arrow) N/A Calculates this expression then switches to the expression above.
↓ (down-arrow) N/A Same drill, but instead goes the the one below.

NOTES

  • The "C" button allows you to clear all expressions from the calculator (you will be given a prompt to confirm).
  • Negative numbers can be entered simply with the "-" button or key. Additionally, a "+" preceeding a number that is not indicative of addition automatically makes it a positive number.
  • Both negative and positive indicators are cumulative. Ex. entering "+--+-++-63" will output as 63.
  • Trigonometric and logarithmic operatings require parentheses to be valid; Ex. "sin(π/3)" is valid, but "sinπ/3" is not.
